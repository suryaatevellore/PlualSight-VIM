Conclusion

Vim has been around for 30 years and, as far as we can tell, will still be around 30 years from now. It's hard to beat Vim's combination of precision and power.

In this series of screencasts, you've learned how to use Vim for basic text editing and have progressed on to configuration, file switching, advanced editing, and plugins.

You've learned to search and read the documentation, which is where much of your future learning will happen.

Operating Vim happens so quickly and smoothly that it's hard to learn techniques just from watching someone use Vim. If you encounter another Vim user in meatspace (which is more likely than you might think), ask them for their favorite tricks.

Keep at it, and before long, you'll be operating Vim as smoothly as an expert, too!
